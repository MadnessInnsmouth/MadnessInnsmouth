Accessibility Statement

Last updated: March 2026

MadnessInnsmouth is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for everyone. We continuously work to improve the user experience and apply relevant accessibility standards.

Our commitment

We aim to conform to the WCAG 2.1 Level AA. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible to people with disabilities and more usable for everyone in general.

Measures we have taken

  • All interactive elements are fully keyboard navigable.
  • Focus is visibly indicated at all times and never suppressed.
  • A skip link is provided to bypass repeated navigation.
  • Navigation menus use aria-expanded, aria-controls, and proper focus management.
  • All non-text content has text alternatives via aria-label or alt attributes.
  • Decorative elements are hidden from assistive technologies with aria-hidden.
  • Colour contrast meets or exceeds WCAG AA ratios throughout.
  • The site respects the prefers-reduced-motion system preference.
  • Semantic HTML5 elements and landmark roles are used throughout.
  • An 18px base font size is used for low vision support.
  • iOS apps are built with native VoiceOver support as a core requirement, not an afterthought.

Known limitations

We are not currently aware of any significant accessibility barriers. If you encounter anything that makes content difficult to access, please let us know.

Feedback and contact

If you experience accessibility barriers on this site or in any of our apps, we want to hear from you. Please open an issue on our GitHub (opens in new tab). We take all reports seriously and will work to resolve them promptly.

Enforcement

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the relevant national enforcement body in your jurisdiction. In the UK this is the Equality and Human Rights Commission (opens in new tab).
