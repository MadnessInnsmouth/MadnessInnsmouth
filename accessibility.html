Accessibility Statement

Last updated: March 2026

MadnessInnsmouth is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We continuously improve the user experience for everyone and apply relevant accessibility standards.

Our commitment

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible to people with disabilities, and how to make user interface content more usable to everyone in general.

Measures taken

  • All interactive elements are keyboard navigable.
  • Focus is visually indicated at all times and never suppressed.
  • A skip link is provided to bypass repeated navigation.
  • Navigation menus include proper ARIA roles, aria-expanded, and focus management.
  • All non-text content has text alternatives via aria-label or alt attributes.
  • Decorative elements are hidden from assistive technologies with aria-hidden.
  • Colour contrast meets or exceeds WCAG AA ratios (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text).
  • The site respects the prefers-reduced-motion system preference.
  • Semantic HTML5 elements and landmark roles are used throughout.
  • iOS apps are built with native VoiceOver support as a core requirement, not an afterthought.

Known limitations

We are not aware of any significant accessibility barriers at this time. If you encounter anything that makes content difficult to access, please let us know.

Feedback and contact

If you experience accessibility barriers on this site or in any of our apps, we want to hear from you. Please open an issue on our GitHub or reach out directly. We take all reports seriously and will work to resolve issues promptly.

Enforcement procedure

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the relevant national enforcement body in your jurisdiction. In the UK this is the Equality and Human Rights Commission.
